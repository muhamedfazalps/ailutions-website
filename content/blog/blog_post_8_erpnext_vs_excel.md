# ERPNext vs Excel: When to Make the Switch (UAE Guide)

Excel is great. Until it isn't.

Here's when to switch from Excel to ERPNext — and when to stay.

## When Excel Works Fine

- You have fewer than 5 customers
- You do fewer than 10 transactions per month
- Your team is just you (or you + 1 person)
- You don't need reports
- Nobody makes errors

If all of these are true, keep using Excel. It's free and simple.

## When to Switch

- You have 10+ customers and the list keeps growing
- You do 50+ transactions per month
- Your team is 3+ people entering the same data
- You spend more than 2 hours per month building reports
- You've caught errors that cost you money

If any of these are true, ERPNext will save you time and money.

## The Real Cost of Staying on Excel

A small trading company with 5 people spending 2 hours per week on data entry:

- 5 people × 2 hours × 50 weeks = 500 hours per year
- At AED 50/hour (average salary cost) = AED 25,000 per year

ERPNext implementation costs AED 15,000-40,000 one time.

You save money in year one. Every year after that is pure savings.

## What ERPNext Gives You That Excel Doesn't

1. **One system** — Everyone sees the same data
2. **Automation** — Invoices, reports, reminders happen automatically
3. **Accuracy** — No more copy-paste errors
4. **Visibility** — Real-time dashboard of your business
5. **Scalability** — Add people without adding spreadsheets
